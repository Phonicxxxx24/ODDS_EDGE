OddsEdge is a professional-grade sharp money detection system that monitors soccer betting markets in real-time. By utilizing multiple data sources, algorithmic steam detection, and cryptographic Merkle proof validation, it isolates high-confidence market anomalies and performs transparent paper-trading simulations.

⚡
How It Works Methodology

1. Steam Detection

Steam detection identifies rapid and persistent shifts in implied probability across the consensus feed. When the de-vigged implied probability of a specific outcome changes by >= 5.0% over a minimum of 3 consecutive ticks (persistence streak) in the same direction, a steam signal is generated. This isolates sharp, high-volume betting activity that causes market-wide price drops.

2. Tri-Source Divergence

Tri-Source Divergence identifies structural differences between distinct market types (Consensus, Professional Bookmaker, and Prediction Exchange). By comparing probabilities from TxLINE, Pinnacle, and Polymarket, the system calculates absolute divergence. If any source deviates by >= 5.0% from the other two, the outlier is flagged, signaling a localized price lag or exchange anomaly.

3. On-Chain Verification

To guarantee the authenticity and timing of our signals, each update can be validated against the TxLINE Cryptographic Validation API. The system resolves the specific update transaction, retrieves its Merkle path, and verifies the proof cryptographically. This prevents retrospective data manipulation and proves that our detection happened in real-time.

Data Sources Feeds

OddsEdge aggregates and normalizes three primary feeds to detect anomalies:

  • TxLINE StablePrice: Our primary consensus source. Prices are consumed via a real-time Server-Sent Events (SSE) stream and converted to implied probabilities: Implied Prob = (1 / (price / 1000)) * 100.
  • Pinnacle: Fetched via the OddsPapi client. Pinnacle odds represent the professional market maker standard. Raw odds are de-vigged by removing the overround to calculate fair probabilities.
  • Polymarket: Fetched via the OddsPapi client. Since Polymarket is an exchange-based prediction market, the orderbook back prices (cents) represent the market's implied probability directly (e.g., 52 cents = 52.0% probability). No de-vig is applied.

Confidence Scoring Formula

Each signal is evaluated using a weighted multi-factor scoring formula to determine its confidence rating (0.0 to 1.0):

Confidence = (Magnitude * 0.35) + (Persistence * 0.25) + (Source Agreement * 0.25) + (Timing * 0.15)

The component metrics are defined as follows:

  • Magnitude Score (Weight: 0.35): Evaluates the size of the probability movement. It is calculated as min(abs(movement_pct) / 15.0, 1.0), reaching a maximum score at a 15% probability shift.
  • Persistence Score (Weight: 0.25): Measures the duration of the movement. It is calculated as min(streak_ticks / 10.0, 1.0), reaching a maximum score at 10 consecutive ticks.
  • Source Agreement (Weight: 0.25): Measures the agreement between the three key feeds. Pure steam signals have a default score of 1.0. Tri-source signals score based on feed agreement (e.g. 2 out of 3 agreeing sources = 2/3).
  • Timing Score (Weight: 0.15): Incentivizes early signals. It assigns 1.0 for pre-match signals, 0.6 for in-play signals under the 60th minute, and 0.3 for late in-play signals (60th minute onwards).

Paper Trading Methodology Simulation

To validate signal profitability, the system runs a flat-stake paper-trading simulation:

  • Flat Staking: Every generated signal triggers a simulated bet of $100.00.
  • Odds Taken: The price taken is the decimal odds at the exact millisecond the signal is registered.
  • Auto-Settlement: An autonomous loop polls the TxLINE historical score feed post-match. Once the game finalization flag is observed, outcomes are scored as CORRECT or INCORRECT.
  • P&L Calculation: Wins are settled at stake * (odds_taken - 1.0), while losses deduct the -stake ($100.00).

Technology Stack Architecture

OddsEdge is engineered with a modular, lightweight stack:

  • Python Agent: A persistent background agent orchestrating the real-time SSE stream listener, stateful steam detector, pre-kickoff tri-source divergence scanner, and the auto-settlement results thread.
  • SQLite Database: Persistent relational store operating in Write-Ahead Log (WAL) mode to permit concurrent reads and writes between the agent and Flask API.
  • Flask Backend API: Exposes clean, cached REST endpoints serving active fixtures, signals, equity curves, stats, and on-demand commentary.
  • Vanilla Frontend: A high-performance, single-page dashboard featuring responsive grid systems, responsive mobile cards, and backdrop blur.
  • Chart.js: Powers the dynamic P&L equity curve timeline.
  • Gemini 1.5 Flash: Generates contextual, on-demand AI explanations for signal occurrences.
  • TxLINE Merkle Proofs: Validates event data integrity via the TxLINE validation system.
